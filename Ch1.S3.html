Doubling time

1.3 Doubling time

If b>db>d then we can consider the doubling time of the population. It is denoted by τ\tau and satisfies

N⁢(t+τ)=2⁢N⁢(t).N(t+\tau)=2N(t). (1.5)

We use the solution (1.3):

N⁢(0)⁢e(b−d)⁢(t+τ)\displaystyle N(0){\rm e}^{(b-d)(t+\tau)} =2⁢N⁢(0)⁢e(b−d)⁢t\displaystyle=2N(0){\rm e}^{(b-d)t}
e(b−d)⁢τ\displaystyle{\rm e}^{(b-d)\tau} =2\displaystyle=2
τ\displaystyle\tau =log⁡2b−d.\displaystyle=\frac{\log 2}{b-d}.

(Notice that tt drops out because the growth is exponential.)

Now log⁡2\log 2 is just a number, so [log⁡2]=1[\log 2]=1 and [τ]=[1b−d]=T[\tau]=[\frac{1}{b-d}]=T.

That is, τ\tau is indeed a time; it will have units of years if bb and dd have units of year-1. On the other hand, if b<db<d then we can describe the decay of the population by calculating a “half life”.
