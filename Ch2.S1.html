2.1 Logistic growth

In 1838, Verhulst proposed a model where the population grows in a way that seems exponential for a time, but cannot exceed a maximum value, or “carrying capacity”, KK. The ODE is

d⁢N⁢(t)d⁢t=r⁢N⁢(t)⁢(1−N⁢(t)K).\frac{{\rm d}N(t)}{{\rm d}t}=rN(t)\left(1-\frac{N(t)}{K}\right). (2.1)

The constant rr can be thought of as the difference b−db-d, and we assume it is positive, but the constant KK has no equivalent in (1.2). Let’s begin by looking at dimensions. We need

[d⁢Nd⁢t]=[r⁢N]=[rK⁢N2].\left[\frac{{\rm d}N}{{\rm d}t}\right]=[rN]=\left[\frac{r}{K}N^{2}\right].
